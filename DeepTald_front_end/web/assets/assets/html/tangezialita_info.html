Ideas do not follow a straight path. Within longer speech passages, content slowly drifts away from where it originally started. The patient does not return to the initial topic.

Different phenomena:

  • Dissociation of Thinking (o)
  • Derailment (o)
  • Circumstantiality (o)

Evaluation:

0 = not present
1 = doubtful
2 = mild: The patient deviates slowly from the initial topic, and in a few instances fails to return.
3 = moderate: The patient deviates quickly from the initial topic, and occasionally fails to return.
4 = severe: The patient deviates immediately from the initial topic and never returns.
