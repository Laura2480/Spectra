In depth

BERT, is an advanced language model developed in the field of artificial intelligence. He has been trained on a large body of text to develop a deep understanding of the semantic and syntactic relationships within words and sentences. BERT represents words contextually, considering the surrounding context in which it appears.

The concept of similarity in the context of BERT refers to the ability to evaluate how semantically similar two pieces of text are. In other words, BERT can calculate how much two sentences share similar information or represent related concepts. This ability comes from its contextual understanding of words: if two sentences share similar keywords or grammatical structures, BERT will be able to identify that similarity.

In practice, BERT evaluates the similarity between sentences by calculating a sort of "similarity score". This score reflects how much the sentences overlap in terms of meaning. For example, if two sentences deal with the same topic or express similar concepts, BERT will assign them a higher similarity score. This process is of great use in many applications, such as information retrieval, semantic search, and context understanding in search engines and natural language processing systems.

However, the potential of BERT goes beyond similarity assessment. One notable application is sentiment analysis. Here, BERT demonstrates his ability to understand not only the literal meaning of words, but also the emotional undertones they carry.

In summary, BERT uses its contextual understanding of language not only to assess similarity between texts, but also for sentiment analysis. This skill opens up a wide range of applications, from advanced natural language processing to creating tools that can understand and respond to the nuances of human emotion expressed through text.
